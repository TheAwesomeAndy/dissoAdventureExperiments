\begin{abstract}
This dissertation introduces ARSPI-Net (Affective Reservoir-Spike Processing and Inference Network), a staged neuromorphic framework for extracting structured, interpretable, multi-scale representations from clinical electroencephalography (EEG). The architecture comprises a fixed-weight Leaky Integrate-and-Fire spiking reservoir, a binned spike count temporal coding bottleneck (BSC$_6$), and a graph-organized spatial analysis layer, producing a representation in which every intermediate variable---membrane trajectories, spike rasters, temporal bin activations, dynamical descriptors, graph-topological metrics, and structure--function coupling indices---is a named, physically grounded quantity traceable to the input event-related potential (ERP) waveform.

The framework is validated on the SHAPE Community dataset ($N = 211$ subjects, $34$ channels, three affective conditions) and introduces a four-level interpretability taxonomy for neuromorphic EEG: temporal traceability (pre-reservoir temporal bins aligned with the BSC$_6$ window structure recover Late Positive Potential amplitude with $|r| = 0.82$; the post-reservoir BSC$_6$ spike-count representation preserves this alignment at $|r| = 0.23$ while gaining condition-specific temporal differentiation), geometric transparency (subject-to-condition variance ratio $\rho = 7.2$, centering gain $+13$ to $+21$ percentage points across seven representations including EEGNet), dynamical characterization (seven named spike-domain descriptors align with classical ERP scalars; the mean amplitude descriptor recovers LPP amplitude at $|r| = 0.82$ per channel, with strongest alignment $r = 0.837$ at Channel~31), and systems-level correspondence (structure--function coupling $\kappa = 0.22$, $p < 0.001$). A head-to-head comparison demonstrates that EEGNet's gradient saliency peaks outside the classical ERP window ($402$--$691$~ms) while ARSPI-Net's learned attention peaks within it ($176$--$254$~ms) with condition-specific differentiation aligned to established ERP components.

The principal discoveries include: (1) subject-covariance entanglement---a universal geometric property of affective EEG where subject and condition share identical principal directions, rendering PCA-based nuisance regression provably lossy; (2) a graph-propagation regime boundary for small dense electrode graphs; and (3) layer-specific clinical sensitivity, where different psychiatric disorders (SUD, GAD, PTSD, ADHD) are most detectable in different representational layers. To our knowledge, ARSPI-Net is the first neuromorphic EEG architecture achieving intrinsic interpretability at all four levels within a single pipeline, positioning it for clinical translation where black-box models cannot serve.
\end{abstract}
